Earthquake Relief in Turkey and Syria


Offering a variety of wellness resources to help people manage their physical and emotional well-being during and after an earthquake.
Providing donation links as well as important information to those who want to help from farther away.

See what NYU is doing to help

It is okay to feel:



  • Shock, denial, or disbelief.

  • Confusion, difficulty concentrating.

  • Anger, irritability, mood swings.

  • Anxiety and fear.

  • Guilt, shame, self-blame.

  • Withdrawing from others.

  • Feeling sad or hopeless.

  • Feeling disconnected or numb.


    • It is okay to experience:



  • Insomnia or nightmares.

  • Fatigue.

  • Being startled easily.

  • Racing heartbeat.

  • Edginess and agitation.

  • Aches and pains.

  • Feeling sad or hopeless.

  • Muscle tension.
